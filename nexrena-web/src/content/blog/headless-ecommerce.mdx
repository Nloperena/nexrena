Headless ecommerce separates the storefront from the backend. Your product data and checkout live in Shopify, Commerce Layer, or a custom API. Your frontend is a separate app — fast, customizable, and decoupled from platform constraints.

## Benefits

- **Speed** — Static or edge-rendered frontend. Sub-second loads.
- **Flexibility** — Design and UX aren't limited by theme. Custom checkout, custom flows.
- **Omnichannel** — Same backend, multiple frontends. Web, app, kiosk.

## Trade-offs

- **Complexity** — Two systems to maintain. More moving parts.
- **Cost** — Development is higher. Hosting may be too.
- **Integrations** — Some platforms assume monolithic. Headless requires API-first thinking.

## When It Makes Sense

- **Performance matters** — You're losing conversion to slow pages.
- **Custom experience** — You need flows that themes can't deliver.
- **Multi-channel** — You're selling beyond the web.
