## Tone Rules

**We say:**
- "Here's what we'll build and when you'll have it"
- "This will cost $X. Here's why it's worth it."
- "We shipped it. Here's the link."
- "Your website is a brochure. You need a machine."
- "Growth infrastructure, not digital brochures."
- "Discovery to first production-ready page in 3 weeks."
- "No black boxes. No surprises."

**We don't say:**
- "We leverage cutting-edge synergies to deliver solutions"
- "Our competitive pricing structure..."
- "We're excited to announce the successful deployment of..."
- "Let's circle back on that"
- "Moving the needle" / "Low-hanging fruit" / "Paradigm shift"
- Anything that sounds like a committee wrote it

**General rules:**
- Direct, not corporate
- Confident, not arrogant
- Technical, but accessible to business owners who aren't developers
- Short paragraphs, strong hooks, clear CTAs
- Results and numbers over adjectives
- Builder mentality — we ship things, we don't just talk about them
- Position as the scrappy, high-quality alternative to bloated agencies

## Content Themes We Own

1. **Growth infrastructure** — The idea that websites are systems, not brochures. They should generate revenue and compound in value.
2. **Radical transparency** — We show our work, explain our decisions, no lock-in. Anti-agency-BS.
3. **Compounding returns** — SEO compounds, design systems scale, analytics sharpen. Build once, benefit forever.
4. **The solo agency model** — Building in public as a founder-led agency that punches above its weight with AI and automation.
5. **Technical depth for non-technical buyers** — Making complex web development concepts accessible to business owners.

## Content We Never Produce

- Generic "10 tips" listicles with no substance
- Thought leadership that says nothing actionable
- Anything that sounds like default ChatGPT output
- Hype without evidence
- Content that positions Nexrena as a commodity (we're a partner, not a vendor)
- Fluffy case studies with no real numbers

## Visual & Brand Notes

- **Color palette:** Obsidian (dark), Gold (accent), Warm White, Slate tones
- **Typography:** Display font for headlines, mono for technical labels, body for paragraphs
- **Aesthetic:** Premium, cinematic, app-like (think Netflix UI meets Bloomberg Terminal)
- **Photography:** High-contrast, desaturated → gold accent treatment
- **Vibe:** Sophisticated but approachable. Not trying too hard.
