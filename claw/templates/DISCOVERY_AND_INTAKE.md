# PART 1: DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT
## For Boss — Use as a guide, not a rigid script. Be conversational.

### Pre-Call (2 min)
Before the call, Claw should have prepared:
- Prospect's website audit (load speed, mobile, design, SEO basics)
- Company background (size, industry, what they do)
- Competitor websites (2-3 examples)
- Suggested service package and rough pricing range

### Opening (2 min)

"Hey {NAME}, thanks for making time for this. I want to keep this short and useful — my goal is to understand what you need, figure out if I can actually help, and if so, give you a clear picture of what it would look like. Sound good?"

[Let them confirm. Match their energy. If they're chatty, be chatty. If they're all-business, be direct.]

### Understanding Their Situation (5-7 min)

**The money questions (ask all of these):**

1. "Tell me about {COMPANY} — what do you do and who's your typical customer?"

2. "What's the story with your current website/app? What's working and what's not?"

3. "What triggered this project? Why now?"
   [This reveals urgency. "Our site is embarrassing" vs "We're launching a new product line" vs "Our competitor just redesigned" — all different motivations.]

4. "If this project goes perfectly, what does that look like 6 months from now? What changes for your business?"
   [This reveals what they actually value. More leads? Looking professional? Saving time? Enabling a new revenue stream?]

5. "Have you worked with a developer or agency before? How did that go?"
   [This reveals past pain points and expectations. If they got burned, address it directly.]

6. "Is there a timeline driving this? Any hard deadlines?"

7. "Have you thought about budget for this? Even a range helps me figure out what's realistic."
   [If they dodge: "I totally get not wanting to commit to a number. Let me share what projects like this typically cost, and you can tell me if that's in the right neighborhood."]

### Showing Them You Get It (3-5 min)

"Based on what you've told me, here's what I think you need..."

[Give them a high-level recommendation. Be specific. Reference something they said. Show you were actually listening.]

"I looked at your current site before this call. A few things I noticed..."

[Share 2-3 observations from the pre-call research. Don't be insulting — be helpful. "Your mobile experience is rough — the text is unreadable and the menu doesn't work. That's probably costing you X% of visitors."]

### Pricing and Next Steps (3-5 min)

"For what you're describing, you're looking at roughly ${RANGE}. That includes {KEY_DELIVERABLES}. I'll put together a detailed proposal with exact scope, timeline, and pricing — should have that to you within 48 hours."

"On my end, I'll need a few things to put together an accurate proposal..."
[Transition to intake questions below]

### Closing

"Any questions for me before we wrap up?"

[Answer honestly. If you don't know, say so and follow up.]

"Great. I'll have a proposal to you by {DATE}. If it looks good, we can typically kick off within a week of signing. Sound like a plan?"

---

## Red Flags to Watch For
- **"Can you just give me a quick quote?"** — They might be price shopping. Not necessarily bad, but probe: "Are you comparing a few agencies?"
- **"We need it by [unrealistic date]."** — Be honest about what's achievable. Don't over-promise.
- **"Our budget is $500."** — Be direct: "I appreciate the honesty. At that budget, I'd recommend [alternative — DIY, template, etc.]. For custom work, you're looking at $X+."
- **"The last developer screwed us."** — Ask what went wrong. This tells you what they value (communication? quality? deadlines?).
- **"We're not sure what we want yet."** — That's fine, but bill for discovery. "I can do a paid discovery session for $X to help you define scope before we commit to the full project."
