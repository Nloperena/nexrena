# Financial Operations — Money In, Money Out

## Revenue Targets

### Year 1 Milestones

| Milestone | Revenue | What It Takes |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Survival | $60k | 4–6 projects at $10–15k |
| Stability | $120k | 8–10 projects + 1 retainer |
| Growth | $200k+ | 10+ projects + 2–3 retainers |

Retainers are the goal. Projects pay bills. Retainers build wealth.

### Unit Economics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Average project value | $15,000–$25,000 |
| Average retainer | $4,000/month |
| Gross margin | 70–80% (solo) / 50–60% (with contractors) |
| Net margin | 40–50% (solo) / 25–35% (with team) |
| Client lifetime value | $30,000–$60,000 (project + retainer upsell) |

### Revenue Math

To hit $200k/year:
- **Option A:** 13 projects at $15k = $195k
- **Option B:** 6 projects at $15k ($90k) + 2 retainers at $5k/mo ($120k) = $210k

Option B is better. Less hustle, more predictability.

## Cash Flow Rules

### Money In
- **Invoice immediately** after milestone completion (don't wait)
- **50% deposit** on every project before work starts (non-negotiable)
- **Retainers billed on the 1st**, due by the 5th
- **Chase late payments** at 7 days with a friendly reminder, 14 days with a firm one

### Money Out
- Keep 3 months of operating expenses in reserve before spending
- Pay contractors within 7 days of invoice (build loyalty)
- Only buy tools you use daily. Cancel everything else monthly.

### The Cash Flow Calendar

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1st | Send retainer invoices |
| 5th | Follow up on unpaid retainers |
| 15th | Review accounts receivable — who owes you? |
| 25th | Review next month's pipeline — enough work coming? |
| Monthly | Reconcile expenses, review profit margin |

## Tax Basics (US / Florida)

- **No state income tax in Florida** — this is an advantage
- **Self-employment tax:** ~15.3% on net earnings (Social Security + Medicare)
- **Federal income tax:** Marginal rates on top of SE tax
- **Quarterly estimated taxes:** Due Jan 15, Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15
- **Set aside 25–30% of every payment** into a separate savings account for taxes
- **Track all business expenses** — they reduce taxable income
- **Deductible expenses:** Software, contractors, home office, internet, phone, meals (50%), travel, education

### The Tax Savings Account Rule
Every time a client payment hits your account, immediately transfer 30% to a separate savings account labeled "Taxes." Don't touch it until quarterly payments are due.

## When to Hire Contractors

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| You're turning down projects because of capacity | Hire a dev contractor |
| You're spending 10+ hours/week on content writing | Hire a copywriter |
| Design is your bottleneck | Hire a UI/UX contractor |
| You have 3+ active retainers | Hire a junior to handle maintenance tasks |
| Revenue consistently above $15k/month | Start building a bench of 2–3 reliable contractors |

### Where to Find Contractors
- **Toptal** — Vetted, expensive, reliable
- **Contra / Arc.dev** — Mid-range, good for dev
- **Your network** — Best option. Ask other agency owners.
- **Upwork** — Last resort. Quality varies wildly.

### Contractor Rules
- Always have a signed contractor agreement (scope, rate, IP assignment)
- Pay promptly — good contractors have options
- Start with a small paid test project before committing to a big engagement
- Never promise a contractor ongoing work you can't guarantee

## When to Raise Prices

Raise prices when 3 of these 5 are true:
1. You're booking out 6+ weeks in advance
2. Your close rate is above 50%
3. Clients aren't pushing back on pricing
4. You haven't raised in 6+ months
5. You're consistently delivering above-scope results

Raise by 15–25% at a time. Apply new pricing to new clients only — honor existing agreements.
