# Sales Playbook — Get Clients Today

## Step 1: Find Prospects (15 min/day)

### Where to Look

**LinkedIn Sales Navigator** (free trial works)
- Search: "VP Marketing" OR "Director Marketing" OR "Founder" OR "CEO"
- Filter: 10–200 employees, Manufacturing / Professional Services / E-Commerce
- Location: Orlando metro first, then Florida, then US

**Google Maps**
- Search: "manufacturing company Orlando" or "law firm Kissimmee"
- Open their website. If it looks bad, they're a prospect.

**Clutch / DesignRush / G2 Competitor Lists**
- Look at agencies listed there. Visit their clients' websites. If the sites are mid, those clients need an upgrade.

**Local Networking**
- Orlando Tech Association, CFEC, local BNI chapters
- Go where business owners go. Not where marketers hang out.

### Qualification Checklist

Before you reach out, check these boxes:

- [ ] Website looks outdated or loads slowly
- [ ] Company has 10+ employees (check LinkedIn)
- [ ] B2B or high-value B2C (not restaurants, not solo freelancers)
- [ ] They have budget signals (office, employees, established brand)
- [ ] You can articulate a specific problem you'd fix

## Step 3: The Discovery Call (15–30 min)

### What to Ask

1. What's your current website doing for you in terms of leads?
2. Where does most of your business come from today?
3. What's changed recently — new product, new market, growth goals?
4. Have you worked with agencies before? What went wrong?
5. If we could fix one thing about your digital presence, what would it be?

### What to Listen For

- "We get no leads from the website" — they need web design
- "We're invisible on Google" — they need SEO
- "We tried an agency and got burned" — emphasize transparency and results tracking
- "We don't have time for this" — they need full-service

### End the Call With

> "Based on what you've shared, I think we can help. Let me put together a quick proposal with scope and pricing. I'll have it to you by [date, within 48 hours]. Does that work?"

## Step 4: Send the Proposal

Use the template in `Founding Documents/Nexrena_Client_Proposal_Template.pdf`.

### Proposal Structure

1. **Executive Summary** — What their problem is (their words, reflected back)
2. **Approach** — How you'll solve it (phases, not features)
3. **Timeline** — 6–12 weeks for project, ongoing for retainer
4. **Investment** — Use the pricing from the packages
5. **Case Study** — One relevant proof point
6. **Next Steps** — Sign, deposit, kickoff date

### Pricing Rules

- Always price the value, not the hours
- Minimum project: $10,000
- Minimum retainer: $3,000/month
- 50% deposit upfront, 50% on delivery (projects)
- Monthly billing on retainers, payable in advance

## Step 5: Close the Deal

### Follow-Up Cadence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 0 | Send proposal |
| 2 | "Just checking — any questions on the proposal?" |
| 5 | Share a relevant blog post or case study |
| 10 | "Want to hop on a quick call to walk through it?" |
| 20 | Final nudge: "Still interested? No pressure either way." |

### Common Objections

**"Too expensive"**
> "I understand. The question is what's it costing you now — in lost leads, slow site, competitors outranking you? Forzabuilt's ROI paid for the project in 3 months."

**"We have an internal team"**
> "That's great. We work alongside internal teams all the time. We bring the specialized infrastructure — performance, SEO architecture, conversion optimization — so your team can focus on what they're best at."

**"We need to think about it"**
> "Absolutely. What specifically would help you make the decision? Happy to provide additional info or adjust scope."

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> "We can absolutely adjust scope. What's most important to you? We can phase the work so you see results before committing to the full engagement."
