# Referral & Repeat Business System

Acquiring a new client costs 5–7x more than retaining one. Your best future clients come from your current ones.

## The Retainer Upsell (Project → Recurring)

Every project client should become a retainer client. Here's when and how to pitch it.

### When to Pitch

**The handoff call** (project delivery): This is the moment of maximum satisfaction. Don't waste it.

### The Script

> "So we're live, results are looking great. Here's something I want to mention — the site is performing well right now, but the real gains come from the next 6 months. SEO compounds. Conversion testing compounds. Without ongoing optimization, you'll plateau.
>
> Most of our clients transition into a growth retainer after launch. It covers ongoing SEO, content, speed monitoring, and conversion testing. We have options starting at $3,000/month.
>
> Want me to put together a proposal for what that would look like for [Company]?"

### Why It Works
- They just saw results — momentum is high
- You're framing it as protecting their investment
- You're not asking them to buy something new — you're asking them to continue what's working

### Retainer Upsell Rates to Target
- **Year 1 goal:** Convert 30% of project clients to retainers
- **Year 2 goal:** Convert 50% of project clients to retainers

## The Referral System

### When to Ask

**30 days after launch** — results are visible, client is happy.

Don't wait 6 months. The window of enthusiasm is short.

### How to Ask

**Email template:**

> Subject: Quick question
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Really glad to see [specific result — "traffic up 40%" or "the new site converting well"].
>
> Quick question — do you know anyone else in [their industry or adjacent] who might benefit from what we did for you? We're always looking for companies at a similar stage.
>
> No pressure at all. But if someone comes to mind, a warm intro would mean a lot.
>
> Thanks,
> [Your name]

**On a call (even better):**

> "By the way, [Name] — if you know anyone who's struggling with their website or digital presence, we'd love an intro. We treat every referral like they're our most important client."

### Making It Easy

People want to refer but it's friction. Remove the friction:

- "Just a quick email intro is perfect — I'll take it from there"
- "You can CC me on an email to them, and I'll follow up"
- "I can send you a 2-sentence blurb you can forward"

### The Referral Follow-Up

When you get a referral:

1. Thank the referrer immediately (same day)
2. Contact the referred prospect within 24 hours
3. Open with: "Hi [Name], [Referrer] suggested I reach out — they mentioned you might be exploring [topic]. We recently worked with them on [project] and saw [result]."
4. After the referred project closes (or doesn't), update the referrer: "Just wanted to let you know we connected with [Name]. Thanks again for the intro."

### Referral Incentives (Optional, Later)

When revenue is stable, consider:
- **10% referral fee** on first project (paid after client pays you)
- **One month free** on their retainer for every referred client that closes
- **Gift** — A nice bottle, a handwritten note, a donation to their preferred charity

Start with just asking. Add incentives when you have predictable revenue.

## Client Expansion Opportunities

Every client has multiple upsell paths. Map them at the start.

| Current Service | Natural Upsell | When to Pitch |
|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Web design | SEO retainer | At handoff |
| Web design | Content creation | Month 2 |
| SEO | Website redesign | When site becomes the bottleneck |
| SEO | Paid media management | When organic is performing and they want to scale faster |
| Retainer (essentials) | Retainer (growth tier) | After 3 months of results |
| Any service | Analytics dashboard | When they ask "how do we know it's working?" |

### The Quarterly Business Review

For retainer clients, schedule a quarterly business review (QBR):

1. **Recap results** — What you delivered, metrics, wins
2. **Show the roadmap** — What's planned for next quarter
3. **Surface new opportunities** — "Based on what we're seeing, here's what would move the needle next"
4. **Ask for feedback** — "Anything we could do better?"

QBRs serve three purposes:
- **Retention** — Client sees the value, stays on retainer
- **Expansion** — You surface new upsell opportunities naturally
- **Referral** — Happy client in a review meeting is primed for "know anyone else?"

## Preventing Churn

| Warning Sign | What to Do |
|-------------|------------|
| Client stops responding to emails | Call them. "Hey, just checking in. Everything okay?" |
| They question an invoice | Walk through the value delivered. Don't get defensive. |
| Results plateau | Proactively present new ideas. Don't wait for them to ask. |
| New marketing hire at client | Schedule intro call. New hires often change agencies. |
| They mention budget concerns | Offer to adjust scope before they cancel entirely |

### The Save Script

When a client hints at cancelling:

> "I totally understand budget constraints. Before we make any changes, can we look at what's working and what we can adjust? I'd rather scale back scope and keep the momentum than stop entirely and lose the compounding gains."

Downgrade before you lose. A $3k client is better than a $0 client.

## Revenue Compounding Model

| Month | New Projects | Active Retainers | Monthly Revenue |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 1 × $15k | 0 | ~$7,500 (deposit) |
| 3 | 1 × $15k | 1 × $4k | ~$11,500 |
| 6 | 1 × $15k | 2 × $4k | ~$15,500 |
| 9 | 1 × $15k | 3 × $4k | ~$19,500 |
| 12 | 1 × $15k | 4 × $4k | ~$23,500 |

By month 12 with 4 retainers, you're at $16k/mo recurring + project income. That's $190k+ annual run rate.

This is why retainer conversion is the most important metric in the business.
