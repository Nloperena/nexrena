# LinkedIn Playbook — Your Primary Sales Channel

LinkedIn is where B2B buyers discover agencies. Every post is a micro-pitch. Every comment is a warm intro.

## Content Strategy

### The 5 Content Pillars

Post from these themes on rotation:

| Pillar | What It Does | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Results | Proves you deliver | "How we got a manufacturer 140% more leads in 90 days" |
| Insight | Shows you're an expert | "Most B2B sites fail because they're designed for the company, not the buyer" |
| Process | Shows how you work | "Here's our 3-phase web rebuild process" |
| Opinion | Makes you memorable | "Stop redesigning your website every 3 years. Build a system instead." |
| Behind the scenes | Humanizes the brand | "Just finished a technical audit. Here's what we found." |

### Post Frequency

**Minimum:** 3x per week (Mon, Wed, Fri)
**Ideal:** 5x per week (daily weekdays)

### Best Times to Post
- 7:30 AM – 8:30 AM ET (catch the morning scroll)
- 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM ET (lunch break)
- Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday get highest engagement

## Post Templates (Copy, Edit, Post)

```

The problem:
→ [specific problem they had]

What we did:
→ [1-2 sentence approach]

The result:
→ [metric 1]
→ [metric 2]
→ [metric 3]

[Takeaway or lesson learned]

If your [industry] website isn't generating leads, it's costing you.

```

```
But the ones that win [what they do differently].

→ [Evidence point 1]

[One sentence conclusion]

Agree or disagree?
```

```

```

## Comment Strategy (5 min/day)

1. Follow 20–30 people in your ICP (VPs Marketing, Founders in target industries)
2. Comment on 5 posts per day — add value, don't just say "great post"
3. If they post about a problem you solve, DM them (see sales playbook)

**Good comment examples:**
- "This is spot on. We see this constantly with manufacturing clients — the site is an afterthought until they realize 80% of their buyers research online first."
- "Strong take. One thing I'd add: [specific insight from your experience]"

**Bad comments:**
- "Great post!" (adds nothing)
- "Check out Nexrena for help with this" (spam)

## Turning Engagement Into Leads

When someone engages with your content (likes, comments, shares):

1. Check their profile — are they in your ICP?
2. If yes, connect with a note: "Thanks for engaging with my post about [topic]. Noticed you're at [company] — we work with a lot of [industry] companies. Happy to connect."
3. After they accept, don't pitch. Share one more piece of value first.
4. Then: "By the way, I took a quick look at [company].com. Noticed a couple things that might help. Want me to share?"

That's it. Value first, pitch second. Always.
